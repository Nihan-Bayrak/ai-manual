Great for everyday tasks: writing, brainstorming, translation, summarization. Start here if you are unsure. Claude 3.5 Sonnet (Anthropic) Ñ Excellent for long, nuanced text. Best for detailed briefings, long-form writing, and careful analysis. Handles large documents well. Gemini 1.5 Pro (Google) Ñ Strong with data, multilingual tasks, and very long documents. Good for research and spreadsheet analysis. Quick Decision Guide: ¥ Everyday writing, emails, brainstorms Õ GPT-4o ¥ Long documents, detailed analysis, nuanced text Õ Claude 3.5 Sonnet ¥ Data-heavy tasks, multilingual, very long files Õ Gemini 1.5 Pro ¥ Quick simple questions Õ GPT-4o mini (faster, lighter) Tip: You can switch models mid-conversation. Use this formula: Prompt = Task + Context + Format ¥ Task: What do you want the AI to do? (summarize, translate, brainstorm, rewrite) Context: What background information does the AI need? (audience, brand, tone, ¥ constraints) Format: How should the output look? (bullet points, table, 3 paragraphs, email ¥ format) Bad prompt: "Write something about packaging trends." Good prompt: "List 5 current packaging design trends in the FMCG sector for 2025. For each trend, write 2 sentences explaining why it matters for brand differentiation. Format as a numbered list. Tone: professional, suitable for a client presentation." 5 Prompt Power Tips: Be specific Ñ "Write 3 tagline options for a premium organic juice brand" beats 1. "Write a tagline" Assign a role Ñ "You are a senior packaging design strategist..." gives the AI 2. perspective 3. Set constraints Ñ "Maximum 100 words" or "Use only simple language" 4. Ask for alternatives Ñ "Give me 3 different versions" helps you compare options Iterate Ñ Your first prompt is a starting point. Core Features for Members 6.1 File Upload & Document Analysis Upload PDFs, Word files, Excel sheets, or images directly into a chat. The AI can summarize, extract key information, translate, or answer questions about uploaded files. Example: Upload a 40-page client briefing Õ Ask: "Summarize the key objectives, target audience, and mandatory design elements from this briefing in bullet points." 6.2 Company Knowledge (@) Type @ in the chat to access shared knowledge folders containing WIN CI documents, guidelines, and resources.This lets the AI answer questions based on your company's own information. Example: Type @BrandGuidelines then ask: "What are the primary brand colors and typography rules for Client X?" 6.3 Web Search Enable Web Search to let the AI access current online information. Useful for market research, competitor analysis, and trend scouting. Example: "Search for the latest sustainable packaging innovations in the European FMCG market from the past 6 months." 6.4 Deep Research Deep Research performs extended, multi-step online research and delivers a compre.hensive report with sources. Takes a few minutes but produces thorough results. Example: "Conduct deep research on Gen Z consumer preferences for snack pack.aging design in Germany. Include statistics and sources." 6.5 Canvas Canvas opens a side-by-side editing space where you and the AI can co-edit text in real time. Perfect for drafting and refining longer documents like proposals, presentations, or briefings. 6.6 Data Analyst Upload CSV or Excel files and ask the AI to analyze data, create charts, identify pat.terns, or generate summaries. Useful for Finance, Project Management, and Strategy. Example: Upload a project tracker Õ "Show me which projects are over budget and by how much. Rank them by severity." 6.7 Projects Projects allow you to organize related chats, files, and prompts in one place. Share projects with colleagues for collaborative work. Example: Create a project called "Client X Relaunch" and keep all related AI chats, uploaded briefings, and generated copy in one location. 7. Go to your profile settings and fill in: Template: "I work at WIN Creating Images (WIN CI), a packaging design agency in Berlin spe.cializing in FMCG. My department is YOUR DEPARTMENT. My role is YOUR ROLE. I primarily work in German/English/both.When I ask for text, default to German/English unless I specify otherwise. Keep responses practical and concise. When suggesting design-related ideas, consider packaging-specific constraints like print production, shelf visibility, and brand guidelines." Example for a Berater: "I work at WIN CI, a packaging design agency in Berlin. I am in Client Service as Etatdirektorin. I communicate with clients in German and internally in German and English. I need help with emails, briefing analysis, meeting summaries, and presentation texts. Keep tone professional, warm, and structured." Example for a Designer: "I work at WIN CI, a packaging design agency in Berlin. I am a Senior Art Director in the Design department. I work in German and English. I need help with concept descriptions, mood board text, presentation copy, and brainstorming. Understand that my work is packaging design for FMCG brands." 9.
